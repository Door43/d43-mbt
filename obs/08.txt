====== Inluwas te Manama si Husi wey ka pamilya rin ======



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-01.jpg}}

Peglihad te masulug ne leg-un, te buyag e si Hakub, insuhu din e ka paburitu rin ne anak ne si Husi eyew egleuy te me kakey rin ne intameng te me ayam dan.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-02.jpg}}

Igkalangeti si Husi te me kakey rin tenged su sikandin ka amana geyinawei te amey ran, wey tenged su nakataheinep si Husi ne egkeyimu sikandin ne Hari dan. Te miggendiyad si Husi te me kakey rin, in-uyan e sikandin te me kakey rin wey imbelegye dan e sikandin diye te egbebeli te me uripen.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-03.jpg}}

Te ware pad nekeuli ka me kakey ni Husi, inggihisi dan e ka kumbale ni Husi wey inlulubi ran e seini te langesa te kambing. Nataman, impakita ran e seini diye te amey ran eyew egsuman-suman sikandin ne inggimatayan si Husi te mahintalunan ne mananap. Ne amana miglungku si Hakub.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-04.jpg}}

Ne in-uyan e te talagbeli te me uripen si Husi diye te Ihiptu. Dakel wey maresen ne nasud ka Ihiptu ne diye te ilis te Weyig ne Nilu. Ne imbelegye e te talagbeli te uripen si Husi diye te datu ne upisyal te gubirnu. Ne insilbiyan ay-ayari ni Husi ka ahalen din, wey impanalanginan sikandin te Manama.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-05.jpg}}

Ne due timpu ne egkeupian ka asawa te ahalen din ne eggulid kandin, piru kene egkeupian si Husi ne egpakasale te Manama. Ne tenged due, nalangetan e sikandin wey imbayungan din e si Husi. Sikan naa ka inderakep e si Husi wey napirisu e sikandin. Ne minsan diye sikandin te pirisuwan, migpalintutuu red iya sikandin te Manama, wey impanalanginan sikandin te Manama.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-06.jpg}}

Ne diye de due si Husi te pirisuwan te daruwe e ne leg-un minsan pad ware sale din. Sabeka ne marusilem, nakataheinep e si Paraun ne Hari te me matig-Ihiptu. Daruwa ka taheinep din wey nakapabenga-benga amana seini kandin. Ne warad iya minsan sabeka te me talagtambag din ka egpakalalag kandin ke nekey ka igpasabut te me taheinep din.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-07.jpg}}

Imbehayan te Manama si Husi te katuenan te pegsabut te taheinep. Purisu impaangey e sikandin ni Paraun puun te pirisuwan. Ne inlalahan e sikandin ni Husi te igpasabut te taheinep din te migkahi, "Egbehey ka Manama te matubung ne peg-ugpe seled te pitu ne leg-un. Ne egsinundul e degma ka bitil selet te pitu ne leg-un."



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-08.jpg}}

Ne amana nahale si Paraun ki Husi, sikan naa ka in-alam din e si Husi ne igkarangeb ne amana ne maresen ne etew diye te intiru ne Ihiptu!



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-09.jpg}}

Joseph told the people to store up large amounts of food during the seven years of good harvests. Then Joseph sold the food to the people when the seven years of famine came so they would have enough to eat.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-10.jpg}}

The famine was severe not only in Egypt, but also in Canaan where Jacob and his family lived.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-11.jpg}}

So Jacob sent his older sons to Egypt to buy food. The brothers did not recognize Joseph when they stood before him to buy food. But Joseph recognized them.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-12.jpg}}

After testing his brothers to see if they had changed, Joseph said to them, “I am your brother, Joseph! Do not be afraid. You tried to do evil when you sold me as a slave, but God used the evil for good! Come and live in Egypt so I can provide for you and your families.”



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-13.jpg}}

When Joseph’s brothers returned home and told their father, Jacob, that Joseph was still alive, he was very happy.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-14.jpg}}

Even though Jacob was an old man, he moved to Egypt with all of his family, and they all lived there. Before Jacob died, he blessed each of his sons.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-08-15.jpg}}

The covenant promises that God gave to Abraham were passed on to Isaac, then to Jacob, and then to Jacob’s twelve sons and their families. The descendants of the twelve sons became the twelve tribes of Israel.
